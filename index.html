Exploring Walkability in NJ Through Machine Learning

This study demonstrates the potential of leveraging machine learning techniques to evaluate and predict walkability across diverse urban and suburban environments in New Jersey. By applying classification and regression models to the EPA Smart Location Database, the key determinants of walkability have been identified, including transit accessibility, intersection density, and residential density.

Global Terrorism: Insights Through Data Visualization in R

This research explores the complexities of terrorism, defined broadly as the use or threat of violence to achieve political, ideological, or religious goals, focusing on its historical trends, regional dynamics, and the socio-political and economic factors driving its propagation. Leveraging data from the Global Terrorism Database and supported by a comprehensive literature review, this study identifies key patterns in global terrorism, including its geographic shift from the Americas and Europe in the 1970s to South Asia, the Middle East, and Africa in recent decades.

Price Prediction in R

In this project, I performed feature engineering and predictive data modeling to predict the prices of Airbnb rentals in New York City. The price prediction was done using OLS linear regression, Random Forest, XG Boost, and Leaps/ Lars. While looking at several different models, it was concluded that the best models are the OLS linear regression model and the xgboost model. However, OLS linear regression model gives a little better prediction.
